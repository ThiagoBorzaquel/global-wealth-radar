When most investors think about risk, they think about stock market volatility.

They rarely think about the risk of the country where their wealth lives.

That's a mistake.

## What Is Sovereign Risk?

Sovereign risk is the probability that a government will default on its obligations, impose capital controls, seize assets, or devalue its currency in ways that destroy private wealth.

It's not just a developing-world problem. In 2013, Cyprus froze bank accounts and imposed a mandatory haircut on deposits over €100,000. In 2001, Argentina froze accounts during its debt crisis.

**Any country can do this.**

## The Four Dimensions of Sovereign Risk

### 1. Fiscal Risk
Is the government spending far more than it collects? High fiscal deficits lead to debt monetization (printing money → inflation) or austerity.

**What to watch**: Debt/GDP ratio, primary deficit, interest coverage.

### 2. Monetary Risk
Can the central bank maintain currency stability? Runaway inflation destroys savings, bonds, and real estate simultaneously.

**What to watch**: Inflation rate, money supply growth, central bank independence.

### 3. Political Risk
Will property rights be respected after the next election? Populist governments sometimes resort to wealth taxes or capital controls.

**What to watch**: Property rights index, rule of law, political stability scores.

### 4. External Risk
Does the country have enough foreign reserves to defend its currency?

**What to watch**: Foreign reserves / import cover ratio, current account balance.

## How to Hedge Sovereign Risk with ETFs

You can't eliminate it, but you can diversify away from it.

- **VTI**: US-domiciled, US assets
- **VXUS**: US-domiciled, international assets (legal diversification)
- **SGOL**: Physical gold stored in Swiss vaults
- **GLD or IAU**: Gold held in US vaults

For large portfolios: consider accounts in Singapore or Switzerland-domiciled funds.

## The Nuclear Hedge

A small portion in physical precious metals held outside the banking system — home safe, safety deposit box in a foreign jurisdiction, or a vaulting service like BullionVault — provides true independence from any single government's decisions.

## Bottom Line

Your country of residence is a portfolio position you're already holding, whether you think about it or not. Diversify accordingly.
