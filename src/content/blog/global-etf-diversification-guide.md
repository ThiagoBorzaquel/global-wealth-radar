Most American investors have 90%+ of their portfolio in US stocks.

That's not a portfolio. That's a bet on one country.

Here's how to fix it — and why it matters more now than ever.

## Why Global Diversification Matters

The US stock market represents roughly 60% of global market capitalization. Concentrating 90% of your wealth in 60% of the world's available opportunities is a form of home country bias — and it has a cost.

Over the last 30 years, international stocks have outperformed US stocks in multiple decade-long stretches. No one knows which decade we're in. Diversification is your hedge against being wrong.

## The Core Four-Fund Portfolio

The simplest globally diversified portfolio uses four ETFs:

### 1. VTI — Vanguard Total Stock Market ETF
- **What it holds**: 3,700+ US stocks (large, mid, small cap)
- **Expense ratio**: 0.03%
- **Suggested allocation**: 40-50% of equity portion

### 2. VXUS — Vanguard Total International Stock ETF
- **What it holds**: 7,500+ stocks in 40+ countries outside the US
- **Expense ratio**: 0.07%
- **Suggested allocation**: 20-30% of equity portion

### 3. BND — Vanguard Total Bond Market ETF
- **What it holds**: 10,000+ US investment-grade bonds
- **Expense ratio**: 0.03%
- **Suggested allocation**: 10-30% depending on risk tolerance

### 4. BNDX — Vanguard Total International Bond ETF
- **What it holds**: Investment-grade bonds from 50+ countries
- **Expense ratio**: 0.07%
- **Suggested allocation**: 5-15%

## Beyond the Core Four

Once your core is established, consider these satellite positions:

- **GLD (5-10%)**: Gold as inflation and tail-risk hedge
- **VNQ (5%)**: US real estate (REITs)
- **VNQI (5%)**: International real estate
- **EEM or VWO (5-10%)**: Dedicated emerging markets tilt

## The Rebalancing Rule

Set a calendar reminder: **once per year, rebalance back to target weights**.

That's it. Annual rebalancing minimizes tax events and removes emotion from the process.

## Common Mistakes

**Mistake 1: Only buying US because "it's what I know"**
Familiarity is not the same as lower risk.

**Mistake 2: Buying too many ETFs**
You don't need 15 ETFs to be diversified. VTI + VXUS + BND is all you need.

**Mistake 3: Timing international entry**
Dollar-cost average into your target allocation over 6-12 months.

## Bottom Line

A globally diversified ETF portfolio reduces concentration risk, captures growth wherever it happens, and requires minimal maintenance.

**Start with VTI + VXUS in a 60/40 ratio.** Add bonds as your risk tolerance dictates. Review once a year.
